About Me

I am a third year Computer Science major at Illinois Institute of Technology. I am interested in front-end and web application development. My interest in science first began when I started doing science projects for school. I really enjoyed putting effort into my projects to make them stand out more. Still today, I value adding visual details to my work whenever I can. I realized that I could combine my interest in computer science and design with front-end development.

I am originally from Istanbul, Turkey. Istanbul was once capital of Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman Empires. I grew up visiting historically diverse landscapes around the city. I still like traveling and learning about different cultures. Technically, I have lived on four continents so far. I had the chance to spend two years in Egypt. It was really fun to visit the Great Pyramids and listen to interesting stories about ancient times while I was there. Later, I came to the U.S. when I was 14 and I’m trying to live on my own since then. My goal was to get an education and expand my worldview at the same time. I went to high school in NYC and now I am in Chicago for college. Although it is not easy to adapt to different places, I find it interesting to meet new people from different backgrounds. I enjoy exploring a city by getting lost. We have this debate in my home country: “Who knows more: a traveler, or a reader?” I was not sure of the answer so I decided to go with both. I believe it is a wonderful experience to travel and study at the same time.
